What is a Haiku?

A Haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three lines. It is known for its brevity and concise nature. The format of a Haiku typically follows a specific structure: the first line contains five syllables, the second line contains seven syllables, and the third line contains five syllables again, for a total of 17. The concise structure and precise language of a Haiku allow for a powerful expression within a limited space.

So how does this help?

In order to keep the poet honest, this website provides live syllable counting. You may also choose to change words to their synonym if that would be a better fit!
